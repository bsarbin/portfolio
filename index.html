My process

I design because I enjoy helping people. By simplifying workflows and making applications more approachable, I help users become superstars at work and at home. When I design, I'm always evaluating if I'm addressing the most critical problems for users and providing the simplest possible solutions. This mindset also helps me to drive consensus around decisions because I'm always looking out for the user and will champion the best idea no matter where it comes from. I also like to explain my design rationale while working with development teams, including pointing out the strengths and weaknesses in my designs. I've found this practice helps mitigate my natural bias, solve some of the tougher challenges and it creates a strong sense of trust on the team. I often see developer work become more user-centered, improving the overall product.

Some additional highlights of my process:

Clearly define goals & outcomes
Identify biz-objects
Craft a story to highlight value props
Design flows for direct manipulation
Set meaningful constraints to move fast
Drive discussions with concrete wireframes with animations
Make decisions for users so they don't have to
Shave away details until it just makes sense
Raise abstraction to biz-level
Pitch in for last minute polish
Research & validate
Discover
Design
Build, Test, Iterate
Launch
